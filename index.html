About Me

Bridging the gap between Earth sciences and technology

My Journey

I'm a passionate geologist and developer currently pursuing my Master's in Geology at Utah State University. My research focuses on igneous petrology, specifically studying mafic enclaves and their role in understanding magmatic processes.

Beyond geology, I'm deeply interested in technology and its applications in Earth sciences. I enjoy creating web applications and tools that can help researchers and the general public better understand our planet.

Current Research

Mafic Enclaves in Igneous Systems

Investigating the petrogenesis and significance of mafic enclaves in intermediate to felsic igneous rocks, focusing on their role in understanding magma mixing and crustal processes.
